The Problem

Silent task drift under context compression

When AI conversations grow long, the context window fills up. Compression algorithms decide what to keep and what to drop. The problem: they optimize for token count, not task relevance. Critical instructions vanish while the AI confidently continues — unaware it's lost the plot.

The surface looks fine

Compressed conversations produce grammatically correct, confident responses. Standard validity checks pass. Nothing looks wrong.

The foundation is gone

But the pivot message — the instruction that defines what the conversation is about — has been silently dropped. The AI is answering a different question than the one you asked.
