Mathematical Foundations

Core Formulas

Core Contract

$$d_{\text{pre}} \geq k$$

Guarded compaction is certified safe only when the pivot retains its required predecessor depth.

Frontier Feasibility

$$W[k] = -\infty \implies \text{infeasible}$$

If the k-slot frontier is negative infinity, no valid completion exists for that retained context.

Raw Validity

$$\text{raw} = \max(\text{primary\_full}, \text{decoy\_full})$$

Answerability alone can stay high even when pivot identity has silently changed.

Mirage Gap

$$\delta = \text{raw} - \text{pivot\_preservation}$$

Large positive gap indicates a validity mirage regime rather than true semantic stability.

Evidence Boundaries

What the current record does and does not cover

Current evidence combines a small committed replay witness with broader paper-level studies.

  • Replay cards show exact proportions on a deterministic witness with n=3 variants per policy and retention fraction. They are not large-sample statistics.
  • Paper-level results (model counts, incident rates, Wilson CIs) live in the flagship PDF and are intentionally separate from the committed witness.
  • Transparent research snapshot — this page reports the current public record, not a finished claim of generality.
Reproduce

Verification paths

Install and register tropical-mcp, then choose between the minimal smoke path and the fuller reviewer workflow. The public certificate on this page is a comparison target for your local run, not a substitute for the reviewer workflow itself.

  1. Smoke test: confirm the runtime with runtime_info(), then run compact_auto(...) and certificate(...).
  2. Research workflow: run diagnose(...) to inspect feasible slots, capture a context_anchor(...), then compare compact_auto(...), certificate(...), and telemetry_summary(...).
  3. Compare the resulting certificate and replay behavior against the public artifacts on this page.
